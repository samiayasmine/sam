I am Sam! Sam, I am!

Inspiration

The highly popular children's book published August 12th, 1960 was created out of the competitive nature lying in all of us. After a bet Seuss took from his publisher that he could write a children's book using less than 255 words, he pulled off the grand feat of writing it with just 50 different words all together.


Fun Facts

  • In 1965, the children's novel was banned in the People's Republic of China for it's portrayal of early Marxism. The ban was lifted shortly after Seuss' death, in 1991.
  • A federal judge upon receiving a boiled egg in the mail by a disgruntled inmate, used the novel as an inspiration for a court order in 2007. US District COurt Judge James Muirhead said "I do not like them in any style. I will not take them fried or boild. I will not take them poached or broiled. I will not take them soft or scrambed!" The inmate sent the egg in protest of the nourishment conditions.
  • Dr.Seuss is, in fact, no doctor at all. His real name is Theodor Seus Geisel. *face slap*
